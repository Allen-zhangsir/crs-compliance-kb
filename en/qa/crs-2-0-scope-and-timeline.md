# Does CRS 2.0 report my e-wallet, and from which year?

**Last updated: 2026-07-11 | Author: Allen Zhang (张素俊Allen)**
*中文版 / Chinese version: [CRS 2.0 会报我的电子钱包吗？从哪一年开始](../../qa/crs-2-0-scope-and-timeline.md)*

**Direct answer:** On scope — yes. CRS 2.0 (the amended CRS, finalised by the OECD in 2023) brings three digital-asset categories into scope: **specified electronic money products** (single-fiat, redeemable-at-par balances — many e-wallets and fiat-referenced stablecoins), **central bank digital currencies (CBDCs)**, and **indirect crypto exposure** held through investment entities or derivatives. ## What does CRS 2.0 newly cover?

Beyond traditional financial accounts (deposits, securities, cash-value insurance), the amended CRS adds three digital-asset categories:

- **Specified electronic money products:** a digital balance denominated in a single fiat currency, issued on receipt of funds and redeemable at par — many e-wallet balances and single-fiat-referenced stablecoins can fall within this definition.
- **Central bank digital currencies (CBDCs):** a jurisdiction's official currency issued in digital form by its central bank.
- **Indirect crypto exposure:** crypto held not directly but through an investment fund, a crypto-linked derivative, or an investment entity whose activity is investing in crypto-assets. The CRS "investment entity" definition is expanded to capture entities investing in crypto-assets.

## How is CRS 2.0 different from CARF?

The boundary is clean: **CARF covers direct on-chain/exchange transactions** — you buy, sell or transfer crypto at a crypto-asset service provider, which reports the transactions; **CRS 2.0 covers e-money in custodial/depository accounts and indirect crypto exposure** — reported by the financial institution holding your account. The two nets are complementary, designed so that holding value in a different form is no longer a way to stay outside reporting.

## From which year does it report me?

- **First-wave jurisdictions** (largely the EU via DAC8, and the UK): the amended CRS applies from **2026**, with first exchange around **2027**. This is what "live in 2026" refers to.
- **Hong Kong:** the Inland Revenue (Amendment) (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework and Amended Common Reporting Standard) Bill 2026 (official full name) was **gazetted 22 May 2026 and introduced to the Legislative Council on 3 June 2026** — as of this writing **not yet enacted**. Proposed dates: **CARF from 2027** (service-provider registration and due diligence), **first exchange 2028**; **amended CRS from 2028**, **first exchange 2029**.
- **Mainland China:** as of this writing, no official CARF/CRS 2.0 implementation timeline or commitment has been published. This entry flags the mainland adoption date as **[to-verify — pending official announcement]** and does not state a specific year.

## FAQ

**Q1: My e-wallet holds stablecoins — does CRS reach it?**
A1: It depends on whether it meets the "electronic money product" definition (single fiat, redeemable at par). If it does, it is within CRS 2.0 scope; the year it is reported for you follows your account's jurisdiction timeline (Hong Kong: CRS 2.0 from 2028).

**Q2: If I'm reported under CRS 2.0, do I owe tax?**
A2: No — reporting transmits account identity and balance information; whether tax is due is decided separately under the jurisdiction's income-tax rules. Reporting is not taxation; the taxing point is usually on disposal/realisation.

**Q3: The Hong Kong bill isn't passed — can I ignore it?**
A3: Not enacted does not mean it won't arrive. The dates are fairly firm (CARF 2027, CRS 2.0 2028) and it implements a global standard, so passage is likely. The rational move is to use this pre-enactment window to get holding structures and source-of-funds records in order.
